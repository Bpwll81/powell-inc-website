III. Tactical Due Diligence Rebuttals

"What if Tier-1 exchanges refuse a Day-1 listing?"

Rebuttal: We concede that a Day-1 listing refusal is likely due to rigorous security and risk assessments. This is not make-or-break. We are happy to work directly with the investor to resolve security concerns and are open to alternative listing methods, provided the protocol remains sovereign.

"Is this just another algorithmic stablecoin risk?"

Rebuttal: Impossible. UGLD is only minted in $5,000\text{ oz}$ intervals and is backed 1:1 by physical gold held in Bail-Free vaults (Texas). There is no fiat backing or speculative circularity; $1\text{ UGLD}$ always equals $1\text{ oz}$ of physical gold.

"How is founder exit/dump risk mitigated?"

Rebuttal: Founders are cryptographically barred from principal liquidation. Only bi-monthly staking rewards are permitted via the Smart Waterfall Protocol, ensuring zero market dump potential from the team.

"What protection exists if the parent entity fails?"

Rebuttal: Assets are held in ring-fenced SPVs. Token holders are legally defined as Senior Secured Creditors with explicit redemption rights to the underlying physical gold.

"Is UNT classified as an unregistered security?"

Rebuttal: No. UNT is a utility token for network governance and fees. It alignment with the Colorado Digital Token Act and EU MiCA standards is a core architectural priority.

"Is the codebase secure and audited?"

Rebuttal: Deployment is cryptographically locked until a Tier-1 audit firm certifies zero critical vulnerabilities. We have allocated specific funds for the investor to manage this audit directly.
